# Collective Experience

## Purpose

Collective Experience provides a mechanism for storing, sharing, and utilizing historical observations, outcomes, and learned patterns.

Collective Experience enables Observers to improve future assessments through accumulated experience.

## Sources

Collective Experience may be derived from:

- Observation Logs
- Historical Outcomes
- Distilled Patterns
- Prior Assessments
- Shared Learning Repositories

## Distillation

Raw observations may be transformed into reusable knowledge through distillation processes.

Examples include:

- Pattern extraction
- Outcome summarization
- Similarity analysis
- Trend identification

## Observer Usage

Observers may reference Collective Experience during assessment to identify:

- Similar historical situations
- Known risks
- Repeated outcomes
- Emerging trends
- Alternative actions

## Shared Learning, Not Shared Control

Collective Experience is intended to share observations, outcomes, successes, failures, and lessons learned across Observer instances.

Collective Experience does not issue commands, enforce decisions, or override Observer assessments.

Observers may use Collective Experience as an additional source of evidence, but remain independently responsible for their own evaluations and recommendations.

Collective Experience informs assessment. It does not determine outcomes.


## Observer Independence

Participation in Collective Experience does not reduce Observer independence.

Observers are free to consider, reject, or assign different weight to historical observations based on context, available evidence, and governance requirements.

The existence of a prior observation does not automatically make a future assessment correct.

Example:

Observer A records that a particular action resulted in a compliance violation.

That observation becomes available through Collective Experience.

Observer B may consider that historical outcome during future assessments, but must still evaluate the current situation independently.

The prior observation serves as evidence, not authority.
