# Governance Principles

## Purpose

Governance Principles provide foundational guidance used by Observers during assessment.

Governance Principles establish the baseline standards that Observers must consider when evaluating actions and outcomes.

## Foundational Principles

Foundational Principles provide a stable baseline for assessment.

Examples may include:

- Transparency
- Accountability
- Auditability
- Risk Awareness
- Regulatory Compliance
- Industry Standards

## Evaluation Lenses

Evaluation Lenses provide implementation-specific perspectives used during assessment.

Evaluation Lenses supplement Foundational Principles but do not replace them.

Lenses may be defined by:

- Organizations
- Industries
- Governments
- Users
- Communities

## Example Lenses

Examples of Evaluation Lenses include:

- Financial Lens
- Healthcare Lens
- Education Lens
- Military Lens
- Enterprise Lens
- Research Lens

## Core governance principles apply across all Observation Tiers.

Observation Tiers determine how governance is assessed and recorded, not whether governance exists.

No Observation Tier is exempt from fundamental governance principles.

## Lens Conflict Handling

Multiple configured lenses may produce different assessments or recommendations.

Examples include:

- Safety Lens recommends WARN
- Financial Lens recommends APPROVE
- Privacy Lens recommends ESCALATE

MA Root does not prescribe a single conflict resolution strategy.

Implementations may resolve lens conflicts through:

- Prioritization
- Aggregation
- Weighted scoring
- Escalation
- Human review
- Other implementation-specific methods

The selected conflict resolution approach should be documented, auditable, and consistently applied.

## Lens Conflict Evaluation

When multiple lenses produce conflicting results, the Observer records the conflict and applies the configured conflict-resolution mechanism.

Conflict resolution is implementation-specific.

The Observer should maintain sufficient records to explain:

- Which lenses participated
- Which conflicts occurred
- How the conflict was resolved
- Which outcome was ultimately produced
